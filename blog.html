The Companies (Amendment) Act 2024: What Every Kenyan Business Must Do Before the Deadline

Kenya's amended companies legislation introduced sweeping changes to beneficial ownership disclosure, annual return requirements, and penalties for non-compliance. With the compliance deadline approaching, many businesses remain unaware of their obligations. This article provides a practical breakdown of what has changed, who is affected, and the steps boards and company secretaries must take immediately.

Under the new regime, every company — including private limited companies and foreign-incorporated entities operating in Kenya — must file a Beneficial Ownership Register with the Registrar of Companies. Failure to do so attracts a fine of up to Ksh. 500,000, with additional daily penalties for continuing non-compliance.
